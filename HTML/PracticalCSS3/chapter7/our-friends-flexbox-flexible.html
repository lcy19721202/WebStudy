Our friends in the wild

Catch up with the zany antics of our wildlife companions. It's nothing but fun when those guys and gals meet up! This endearing movie contains at least 57% of the typical amount of heartwarming friendship and adventure of the average Disney movie.

Fun facts!

  • Our friends in the wild is one of the most expensive animated films ever made.
  • To create all of the authentic sound effects used in the movie, over 11,000 wild animals had to be flown in and recorded in the studio.
  • This included 3,400 Gazelles and 1,100 hippos, all meticulously recorded as part of the savannah choir.
  • The most difficult part of the wildlife recording process was the strong artistic temperament of Zimbar, who played the part of Lauke the Warthog. His demands were so over the top that pat way through he was taken off the project.
  • Ben Affleck was drafted in to finish Lauke the Warthog's parts, at the last minute and at great expense.
  • Our friends in the wild is set in the savannah of Burkina Faso, West Africa.
  • This location was chosen as the setting because there were worries about litigation from the creators of the Lion King. This was aggravated by the fact that Dino Zoozoo, main writer of Our friends in the wild, was cautioned by security for running amok in Disney studios, shouting abusive taunts about the Lion King.
  • The government of Burkina Faso is delighted by having a major motion picture set there.

The story so far

Our story centres around Lauke the Warthog, a plucky young plains dweller who was orphaned at an early age, and raised by a large pride of lions. He remembers little of his past, but he is has grown into adolescence a courageous brave character. His best friend is Pete the Crocodile, a depressive emo with a heart of gold, who looks up to Lauke for encouragement and belonging. Lauke has the heart of a lion, and fiercely stands up for his friends.

Lauke himself looks up to the savannah elders for wisdom and insight in times of trouble. Elijah the Parrot spends most of his time listening to drum and bass on his MP3 player, and may come across as a frazzled old raver, but he has many a wise word to say in difficult situations. And Armani the Lion, former leader of the pride but long since deposed, may seem to have lost his marbles, but sees and hears all.

The movie starts with Lauke and Pete investigating a cry for help from some local gazelle kids, who came across something nasty at the edges of the plains. They have seen dark spirits mobilising, as if the dead themselves have been disturbed from their rest! Who is at the bottom of this disturbance? Their travels open up a whole world of adventure, pitting them against dark supernatural forces, greedy hunters and sinister desert warriors. The key to finding the answers they seek is a chance meeting with the Party flamingos, but can they make them speak sense?
