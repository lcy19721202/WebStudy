My Knights are assembled!

Introduction

At last, I have assembled a glorious force of knights, worthy of being called the knights of Camelot. We have the wisdom and intelligence of Sir Bevedere the Wise. We have the faith and devotion of Sir Galahad the pure. We have the power, courage and incredible strength of Sir Lancelot the Brave. And we have the … Erm … Ummmm … minstrels of Sir Robin the Not-Quite-So-Brave-As-Sir-Lancelot. Let's say a few more words about myself, and my loyal followers!

King Arthur

King Arthur — that's me — is the King of the Britons, a title granted by the fulfillment of a prophecy, whereby he pulled the sacred sword — Excalibur — from the stone, and then had it gifted to him by the Lady of the Lake as the one true King of the Britons. He alone will lead his subjects in the light, and defend his proud country against the raging barbarian hordes of the Saxons.

He has assembled a group of heroes together under the moniker of The Knights of the round table. Their latest quest is to obtain the holy grail, a divine quest handed ot them directly by god. He hates peasents, and is scared of French soldiers. He also has an in-depth knowledge of bird flight speeds.

Sir Bedevere the wise

Sir Bedevere the wise is a great thinker and scientist, brought into King Arthur's knights because of his deep knowledge of science. King Arthur first witnessed his amazing skills when he witnessed Bedevere settling a dispute between some peasants. They were accusing a woman of being a witch and in the process of lynching her. Bedevere stepped in and gave her a fair hearing, scientifically proving that she was in fact a witch by using a logical means. He showed that she weighed the same as a duck, by floating both on water, therefore a witch she must be.

Sir Galahad the pure

Sir Galahad is the most pious and pure of King Arthur's knights, being closest to god, and chosen to be one of the chosen wardens and protectors of the holy grail, at the point that the knights obtain the grail. He is put in most peril during the grail quest when he chances upon Castle Anthrax, a dwelling that houses a convent group comprised of chaste but very frustrated maidens, who are unavoidably attracted to the brave handsome knight. Galahad is eventually saved for the terrible fate of succumbing to their advances by Sir Lancelot.

Sir Lancelot the brave

Sir Lancelot was the bravest, strongest and most fearsome of King Arthur's knights. He was an instrumental figure in most of King Arthur's battles, and unmatched in his sword skills. Arthur was the only one who came close.

In the grail quest, Sir Lancelot has so far succeeded in rescuing Sir Galahad from the advances of the maidens of Castle Anthrax, and answering a distress call from what he believed to be a fair maiden imprisoned against her will by a cruel father in Swamp Castle. After he stormed the castle and fought his way through the grounds and up to the top of the keep, killing several guards and wedding guests in the process, he found out that the distress call was in fact from an effeminate young man. Despite this faux pas, Arthur made some good friends here, namely the Lord of Swamp Castle.

Sir Robin the Not-Quite-So-Brave-As-Sir-Lancelot

Sir Robin is about as much use as a chocolate teapot, or a blow-up dartboard. This not-so-brave knight took part in the grail quest, but unfortunately upheld his reputation, running away at the first sign of danger. As he fled from the first whiff of a battle with a three-headed giant knight, his minstrels penned a fitting tune, chronicling the awe-inspiring way in which their great hero had fled.

Summary

A force to be reckoned with, don't you think? Let us hope that we can carry the wishes of our lord god, and successfully retrieve the holy grail for the muddy proud people of Britain.
